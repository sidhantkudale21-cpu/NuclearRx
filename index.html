S-Value used
— mGy/(MBq·h)
Formula: 1/Teff = 1/Tphys + 1/Tbiol
Teff = (Tphys × Tbiol) / (Tphys + Tbiol)
Single-compartment: Ã = A0 × Teff / ln2
Multi-compartment: Ã = Σ (fi × A0 × Teff,i / ln2) — up to 3 components.
Reverse MIRD: Given target absorbed dose D(Gy), find required administered activity A0.
A0 = Dtarget / (S × Teff / ln2)

⚠ Mandatory QC Tests (AAPM TG-181 / IAEA HS-24)
Constancy: Daily — same source, ±5% of baseline
Accuracy: Annually — NIST-traceable source, ±5%
Linearity: Quarterly — decay or attenuator method, ±5% over clinical range
Geometry: At installation — different volumes/containers
🧪 RP Preparation Guide
Preparation methods, QC tests & shelf life per manufacturer SmPC / USP / Ph.Eur. Always follow your institution's SOPs and current product insert.
📋 General QC Principles (USP <821> / Ph.Eur. 2.7.1)
Visual inspection: Clear, colourless/pale yellow; no particles
pH: 4.5–8.5 for all parenteral RPs (unless stated)
Radiochemical purity (RCP): ≥95% unless product-specific
Radionuclidic purity: Per individual product monograph
Sterility: Retrospective (membrane filtration 0.22 µm); parametric release for PET
Endotoxin: <175 EU/dose or <5 EU/kg body weight
Specific activity: Critical for receptor-binding agents
